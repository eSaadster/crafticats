🚛 Truck Driver Prospecting — Reality Check

CDL drivers, owner-operators, fleet managers — none of these tools are built for it

🦞 Crustdata Best of 3

Can find trucking companies via firmographic filters and monitor fleet operator signals. Watcher API can track logistics firms for job openings or leadership changes. But CDL drivers themselves? No individual driver profiles.

🚀 Apollo.io

Has industry + job title filters. You can search for "Transportation & Logistics" + titles like "Owner Operator," "Fleet Manager," or "Dispatcher." Decent for fleet managers. Individual CDL drivers? Sparse at best.

🎯 Hunter.io

Useless for driver prospecting. Works on company domains to find professional emails — truck drivers don't have company email addresses. Only helps for HR/recruitment at large carriers.

⚡ Verdict: None of the three are built for this. Here's what actually works:

Truck driver data lives in specialized databases: FMCSA MCMIS (public DOT registry with 3.5M+ carriers & owner-operators), DMDatabases, ProspectWallet, and niche CDL recruiting agencies like OTR Driver Leads. For CDL prospecting, you need a dedicated trucking database — not a generic B2B tool. Apollo can get you fleet managers and dispatchers, Crustdata can track logistics company signals, but if you're targeting individual drivers, go to the source: FMCSA data + specialized trucking lists.

Parallel AI vs Exa — for GTM Prospecting

Parallel AI Search vs Exa Search

Web intelligence APIs for AI-powered prospecting agents

VS

⚡ Parallel AI — Winner on accuracy

  • 🎯 48% BrowseComp accuracy — Exa hits 14%. That's not close.
  • 💡 Powers Claygent (Clay's AI research agent) — the de facto GTM standard
  • 💰 $21–192 CPM — cheapest accurate search at scale
  • 🔗 Multi-hop reasoning — finds obscure prospect info others miss
  • 📦 Task API for deep research + Search API for live web context
  • 🔒 SOC-2 Type 2 certified, trusted by enterprise GTM stacks

🔍 Exa — Better for semantic discovery

  • 🌐 Semantic/neural search — finds content by meaning, not just keywords
  • 📄 Websets: builds structured prospect lists from open web in natural language
  • ⚡ Great for content research and thematic discovery
  • 🎓 Strong for finding niche communities, forums, and specialized sources
  • 💸 $402 CPM on BrowseComp vs Parallel's $100 — ~4x more expensive
  • 🚛 Better for finding trucker forums, CDL communities, niche directories
For truck driver prospecting specifically: Exa's semantic search is actually more useful — it can surface trucking subreddits, CDL forums, owner-operator communities, and niche directories that keyword-based search misses. Parallel AI is superior for researching individual companies and decision-makers at scale. Ideal stack: Parallel for prospect enrichment, Exa for community discovery and list-building from niche sources.
05 / Bottom Line

Who Should Use What

For Builders & AI Teams

🦞 Crustdata

You're building an AI SDR, automated enrichment pipeline, or event-triggered outreach system. You have engineering. You need fresh, API-accessible data that doesn't go stale. This is your play.

For Sales Teams & Founders

🚀 Apollo.io

You have a sales team doing manual outbound. You want the biggest database, built-in sequencing, and a Chrome extension that works on LinkedIn. Start here — there's a free tier and 9,000+ reviews backing it up.

For Email Hygiene

🎯 Hunter.io

You already have a list and need to verify it before sending. Or you need to find a specific person's email from their company domain. It's a scalpel, not a sledgehammer — use it as a layer on top of Apollo or Crustdata.
