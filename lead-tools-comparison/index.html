🚛 Truck Driver Prospecting — Reality Check
CDL drivers, owner-operators, fleet managers — none of these tools are built for it
Crustdata Best of 3

Can find trucking companies via firmographic filters and monitor fleet operator signals. Watcher API can track logistics firms for job openings or leadership changes — useful for B2B fleet management SaaS. But CDL drivers themselves? No individual driver profiles in the database.

Apollo.io

Has industry + job title filters. You can search for "Transportation & Logistics" + titles like "Owner Operator," "Fleet Manager," or "Dispatcher." Decent for fleet managers and logistics decision-makers. Individual CDL drivers with verified contact info? Sparse at best.

Hunter.io

Useless for driver prospecting. Works on company domains to find professional emails — truck drivers don't have company email addresses. Would only help if you're emailing the HR/recruitment team at a large carrier.

⚡ Verdict: None of the three are built for this. Here's what actually works:

Truck driver data lives in specialized databases: FMCSA MCMIS (public DOT registry with 3.5M+ carriers & owner-operators), DMDatabases, ProspectWallet, and niche CDL recruiting agencies like OTR Driver Leads. For CDL prospecting, you need a dedicated trucking database — not a generic B2B tool. Apollo can get you fleet managers and dispatchers, Crustdata can track logistics company signals, but if you're targeting individual drivers, go to the source: FMCSA data + specialized trucking lists.

🔍 Exa — Better for semantic discovery
  • 🌐 Semantic/neural search — finds content by meaning, not just keywords
  • 📄 Websets: builds structured prospect lists from open web in natural language
  • ⚡ Great for content research and thematic discovery
  • 🎓 Strong for finding niche communities, forums, and specialized sources
  • 💸 $402 CPM on BrowseComp vs Parallel's $100 — ~4x more expensive for less accuracy
  • 🚛 Better for finding trucker forums, CDL communities, and content signals
For truck driver prospecting specifically: Exa's semantic search is actually more useful — it can surface trucking subreddits, CDL forums, owner-operator communities, and niche directories that keyword-based search misses. Parallel AI is superior for researching individual companies and decision-makers at scale. Ideal stack: Parallel for prospect enrichment, Exa for community discovery and list-building from niche sources.
